Who We Are

Not a charity.
An ecosystem.

"We didn't build Pheel to give people fish. We built it so they could own the whole fishing industry."

Nakuru has no shortage of talent. What it has a shortage of is structure — the kind that turns raw skill into real income, scattered hustle into sustainable business, and isolated creatives into a powerful community.

Pheel Creations Hub was built to be that structure. We're not a training centre that hands out certificates and wishes you luck. We're a full creative and business ecosystem — under one roof, in the heart of Nakuru — combining skills development, business incubation, professional workspace, photography and media services, event production, and community networking.

We run on revenue, not goodwill. That's by design. Because when your model is sustainable, your impact is permanent.

What Sets Us Apart

The Pheel difference.

We're self-sustaining by design

No grants, no donor dependency, no begging. Pheel was built as a business first — one that creates social impact as a direct outcome of its commercial success. When we grow revenue, we grow reach.

One roof, full ecosystem

Our competitors offer one service and send you away for the rest. We give you skills, incubation, workspace, professional services, events, and community all in one place. That integration is our product.

We walk the talk on women

Women's empowerment isn't our tagline — it's our daily reality. The majority of our participants are women, and we've built our programs, schedules, and pricing to actually accommodate their lives.

Nakuru is the point

Most creative economy energy stays in Nairobi. Pheel was built specifically to develop Nakuru's creative economy — not as a branch of something bigger, but as the thing itself. Nakuru doesn't need a Nairobi branch. It needs its own engine.

Certified, credible, connected

NITA registration, ILO partnership, Nakuru County relationships, ASSEK membership — we've built institutional credibility deliberately so that doors open for our community, not just for us.

Impact you can measure

70% of our graduates are employed or running businesses within months of completing training. That's not a hope — it's a metric we track and we're not satisfied until it improves.
